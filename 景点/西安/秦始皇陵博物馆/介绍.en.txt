The Terracotta Warriors and Horses pit was discovered in 1974 and was hailed as the "Eighth Wonder of the World"; a museum was built and opened here in 1979. The three figurine pits are distributed in the shape of a pin, with a total area of ​​more than 20,000 square meters. They contain nearly 8,000 life-size pottery figurines, pottery horses and more than 10,000 bronze weapons. In 1987, the Mausoleum of the First Emperor of Qin (including the Terracotta Warriors and Horses Pit) was included in the World Heritage List. Together with treasures such as bronze chariots and horses, it reveals the glory of the military and sculpture art of the Qin Dynasty.
