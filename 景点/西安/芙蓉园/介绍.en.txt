It was originally a royal forbidden garden in the south of Chang'an City during the Sui and Tang Dynasties. It was the core place for emperors' banquets and imperial examination celebrations in the Tang Dynasty. Now it has been rebuilt to the north of the original site as the "Datang Furong Garden". It is China's first large-scale royal garden-style cultural theme park that fully displays the style of the prosperous Tang Dynasty. It covers an area of ​​1,000 acres and 300 acres of water. There are buildings imitating the Tang Dynasty such as Ziyun Tower and Ladies Pavilion in the park. It also has the world's largest water screen movie. It is a culmination of garden art and Tang culture. You can deeply experience the cultural highlights of the Tang Dynasty such as "Drinking on the Qujiang River".
