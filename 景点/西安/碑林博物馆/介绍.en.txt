It started in the second year of Yuanyou (1087) in the Northern Song Dynasty and was built to collect famous steles such as "Kaicheng Stone Classic". The museum has a collection of more than 4,000 square meters of steles from the Han Dynasty to the Qing Dynasty. It is famous for national treasure-level cultural relics such as the Shitai Filial Sutra, Nestorian Popular Chinese Stele of the Great Qin Dynasty, and the Six Horses of Zhaoling. It is known as the "treasure house of Chinese civilization" and the "ancestral court of calligraphy art".
