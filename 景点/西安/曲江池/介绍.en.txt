It flourished in the Qin and Han Dynasties and flourished in the Sui and Tang Dynasties. It is a famous royal garden and public tourist attraction in the ancient capital of Chang'an. The blue waves here are rippling, and the pavilions are undulating. In the Tang Dynasty, whenever a new scholar passed the imperial examination, he would put his wine glass in the water and float with the current. It was called "Drinking in Qujiang River" and it was one of the "Eight Scenic Spots in Guanzhong". Today's ruins park is centered around a pond, recreating the garden style of the prosperous Tang Dynasty and serving as a great place for citizens and tourists to relax and enjoy sightseeing.
