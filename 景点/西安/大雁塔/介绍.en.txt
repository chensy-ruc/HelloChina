Located in the Daci'en Temple in Xi'an, it was built in the third year of Yonghui of the Tang Dynasty (652). It was built for Master Xuanzang to collect Buddhist scriptures and relics brought back from India. It was originally five stories and later increased to seven stories. It is 64 meters high and is a pavilion-style brick tower with a simple and majestic shape. As a landmark building in the ancient capital of Xi'an, the pagoda contains precious cultural relics such as the Bay Leaf Sutra and Buddha statues. Climbing the pagoda can overlook the entire city and appreciate the atmosphere of the prosperous Tang Dynasty and the great achievements of the spread of Buddhism to the east.
