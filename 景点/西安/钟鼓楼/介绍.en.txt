The Bell Tower was built in the 17th year of Hongwu in the Ming Dynasty and is the largest and best-preserved bell tower in the country. The Drum Tower echoes it and originally had a huge drum to tell the time. Both floors are wooden buildings with double eaves and resting tops. Bells in the morning and drums in the evening once controlled the city's daily routine. Climbing the tower overlooks the fireworks of Muslim Street and the central axis of the city. It is the time coordinate and landmark symbol of the ancient capital of Xi'an.
