Xi'an, known as Chang'an in ancient times, is the capital city with the most dynasties and the longest history in Chinese history. It has a history of more than 3,100 years of city construction and more than 1,100 years of capital construction. The Mausoleum of Qin Shihuang and the Terracotta Warriors and Horses pit are listed as world cultural heritage, and are known as the "Eighth Wonder of the World" for their large scale and lifelike terracotta warriors. The urban layout follows the square system of the Tang Dynasty, and the Ming Dynasty City Wall is the largest and best-preserved ancient city wall in China. The Big Wild Goose Pagoda, Small Wild Goose Pagoda, Bell and Drum Towers and Muslim Street jointly witnessed the multicultural integration at the starting point of the Silk Road. Today's Xi'an not only retains the solemn atmosphere on the historical axis, but also develops high-tech industries and scientific and educational resources. It is a national central city that shines in ancient and modern times.
