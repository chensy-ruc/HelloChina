It was first built in the second year of Emperor Kaihuang's reign in the Sui Dynasty (582) and was originally named Qingxin Temple. It was the ancestral hall of Chinese Buddhism and Esotericism. During the Tang Dynasty, the Japanese monk Kukai came to the Tang Dynasty to seek Dharma here. He studied under Master Huiguo and founded the Shingon Sect after returning to China. Therefore, the temple is revered as the birthplace of the Shingon Sect in Japan. Located on Leyouyuan, the temple's Tang Dynasty-style courtyard is complemented by more than a thousand cherry blossoms, which combines the elegance of exploring ancient ruins and enjoying cherry blossoms in spring.
