The most magnificent palace in the Tang Dynasty, it was built during the Zhenguan period and served as the political center of the Tang Empire for more than 200 years. It covers an area of ​​approximately 3.2 square kilometers, equivalent to three Versailles Palaces. The Hanyuan Hall, Linde Hall and other ruins are magnificent, recreating the grand scene of the "Palace of Thousand Palaces". Today it is the Daming Palace National Heritage Park, which places equal emphasis on archeology and restoration, allowing people to imagine the grandeur of the Tang Dynasty.
