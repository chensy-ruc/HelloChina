The oldest Buddhist temple in Shanghai is said to have been built during the Wu Chiwu period of the Three Kingdoms, before the city of Shanghai was built. Located in a bustling city, the splendid mountain gate complements the surrounding modern buildings. The temple is dedicated to Sakyamuni Buddha, a thousand-year-old well, and inscriptions from past dynasties. It is surrounded by Sanskrit sounds and filled with incense. It is a rare and quiet temple in the city and an important landmark of Buddhist culture in Shanghai.
