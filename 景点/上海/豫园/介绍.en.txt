First built during the Jiajing period of the Ming Dynasty, it was the private garden of Pan Yunduan, the governor of Sichuan. It is known as the "Crown of Famous Gardens in the Southeast". The pavilions, pavilions and pavilions in the garden are scattered in an orderly manner, and the rockeries and ponds complement each other. The "Linglong Jade", one of the three strange stones in the south of the Yangtze River, is the most famous. The entire garden has an exquisite layout and winding paths, integrating the essence of Jiangnan gardening art from the Ming and Qing dynasties. Together with the adjacent City God's Temple and the Old City Wing, it constitutes the most traditional cultural space in Shanghai.
