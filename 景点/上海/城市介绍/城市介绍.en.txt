Shanghai is located at the estuary of the Yangtze River and is China's largest economic center city and international gateway. After its opening in 1843, it quickly emerged as a financial and trade hub in the Far East. The Bund’s “Architecture Expo Group of All Nations” records the century-old history of the concession. Across the Huangpu River, the Lujiazui Financial District outlines the 21st century skyline with landmarks such as the Oriental Pearl Tower and Shanghai Tower. Yuyuan Garden, Chenghuang Temple and Shikumen Lane retain the memory of the Jiangnan water town and the life of modern citizens. As a place of cultural fusion, Shanghai has nurtured Shanghai-style culture, which is both refined and open. Today's Shanghai has the world's busiest container port and Pudong International Airport. It is also at the forefront of China's technological innovation and fashion consumption, and is an international metropolis facing the future.
