The former residence of Du Fu, a poet from the Tang Dynasty, who lived in Chengdu. Du Fu lived here for nearly four years from 759 to 765 AD and left more than 240 poems including "Song of a Thatched House Broken by the Autumn Wind". In the garden, the Zhaobi, main entrance, Daxi, Poetry and History Hall, Chaimen, and Gongbu Temple are arranged on the central axis. The thatched cottage scenic area was rebuilt according to Du's poems. The four characters "Shaoling Thatched Cottage" are engraved on the stele pavilion. It is elegant and profound, and is known as a holy place in the history of Chinese literature.
