Located on the Minjiang River in the Chengdu Plain, it was first built in 256 BC. It was a large-scale water conservancy project led by Li Bing and his son, the prefect of Shu County in the Qin State during the Warring States Period. It is composed of three main bodies: Yuzui diversion embankment, Feishayan spillway and Baopingkou water diversion port. The exquisite design of dam-less water diversion, automatic diversion, sand discharge and flood discharge has turned the Chengdu Plain into a "land of abundance". It still irrigates tens of millions of acres of fertile farmland, making it the originator of the world's water conservancy culture and a world cultural heritage.
