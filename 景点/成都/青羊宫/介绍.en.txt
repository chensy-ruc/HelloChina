It is known as "the first Taoist temple in western Sichuan". According to legend, it was built in the Zhou Dynasty and was originally named Qingyangsi. Emperor Xizong of the Tang Dynasty used this place as his palace when he took refuge in Shu. Later, he issued an edict to change the name and it is still in use today. The Bagua Pavilion in the palace is exquisitely laid out and luxuriously shaped. The bronze-cast green sheep in front of the Sanqing Hall is a treasure of the temple. The treasured engraving of "Tao Zang Ji Yao" is also a rare treasure.
