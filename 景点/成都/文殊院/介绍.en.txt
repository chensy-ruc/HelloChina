A famous Buddhist temple in western Sichuan, it was first built during the Daye period of the Sui Dynasty. It was originally named Xinxiang Temple. After reconstruction during the Kangxi period of the Qing Dynasty, it was renamed Wenshu Monastery. It is known as "the first of the four major Zen forests in the upper and lower reaches of the Yangtze River". The five major halls in the temple are arranged along the central axis and are typical of the Qing Dynasty architectural style in the Western Sichuan Plain. They are simple, spacious, and majestic. The collection of cultural relics is extremely rich, and it can be called a rich treasure house of Buddhist art.
