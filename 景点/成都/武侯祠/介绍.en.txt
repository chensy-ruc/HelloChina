Built in memory of Zhuge Liang, it was named after Zhuge Liang was once granted the title of Marquis of Wuxiang. It is the only ancestral temple in China where both monarch and ministers worship together, and is connected to Huiling (Liu Bei's tomb). The "Three Jue Steles" of the Tang Dynasty, the architectural complex of the Qing Dynasty and the stone carvings of Yue Fei's handwritten "Chu Shi Biao" in the temple are particularly valuable. With green cypresses, red walls and numerous plaques and couplets, it is a holy place to appreciate the culture and spirit of loyalty of the Three Kingdoms.
