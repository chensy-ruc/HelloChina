Beijing is the capital of the People's Republic of China, with a history of more than 3,000 years as a city and more than 860 years as a capital. The north-south central axis connects Yongding Gate, the Forbidden City, Jingshan Mountain to the Bell and Drum Tower, embodying the etiquette thought of ancient capital planning. As the largest existing wooden palace complex in the world, the Forbidden City carries the royal memories of the Ming and Qing dynasties. Hutongs and courtyard houses retain the spatial texture of civilian life in old Beijing. In the process of modernization, the Central Business District, Zhongguancun Science and Technology Park and Olympic Park have demonstrated contemporary vitality. Beijing has seven world cultural heritage sites, including the Great Wall, the Summer Palace, the Temple of Heaven, and the Central Axis. It is a city that combines the heritage of an ancient capital with an international temperament.
