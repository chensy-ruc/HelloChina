Beihai Park, located in the city of Beijing, is one of the oldest and best-preserved imperial gardens in China. It began in the Liao and Jin Dynasties and flourished in the Yuan, Ming and Qing Dynasties. The white tower stands on Qionghua Island and the vast blue waves of Taiye Lake. The nine-dragon wall in the garden is exquisite, the Yong'an Bridge is simple and elegant, and the pavilions and pavilions are well-proportioned. They fully demonstrate the grandeur of northern gardens and the elegance of Jiangnan water towns. They can be called treasures of garden art.
