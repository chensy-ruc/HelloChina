Jingshan Park is located on the central axis of Beijing and was the royal garden of the Yuan, Ming and Qing dynasties. The Wanchun Pavilion in the garden is the center point and commanding height of the capital. From there, you can have a panoramic view of the Forbidden City to the south and the Bell and Drum Tower to the north, giving you a panoramic view of the capital. The Shouhuang Palace in the garden is solemn and solemn. The ancient locust trees where Emperor Chongzhen hanged himself still exist. The natural scenery and historical traces blend together. When visiting the garden, you can also feel the vicissitudes of life.
