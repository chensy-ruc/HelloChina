The model of royal gardens in the Qing Dynasty uses Wanshou Mountain and Kunming Lake as its skeleton and draws on the landscaping art of Jiangnan gardens. The corridor is exquisitely painted, the stone boat is unique, and the Buddhist Incense Pavilion is magnificent. As the most complete preserved garden among the "Three Mountains and Five Gardens", it integrates landscape, architecture, and humanities. It can be called the pinnacle of Chinese classical garden art and is also a world cultural heritage.
