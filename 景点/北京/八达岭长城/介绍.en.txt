The Badaling Great Wall is the most completely preserved and earliest open section of the Ming Dynasty Great Wall. It has a dangerous terrain and strong city defense. It is known as the "Key to the North Gate" in history. Climbing the city wall, you can have a panoramic view of the majestic scenery of giant dragons entrenched in the mountains, and deeply appreciate the exquisiteness of the ancient military defense system. It is also an important cultural landmark to understand the spiritual connotation of "if you don't see the Great Wall, you are not a hero".
