Nanluogu Lane is one of the oldest neighborhoods in Beijing. It was built in the Yuan Dynasty and has a fishbone-shaped alley pattern. This place once housed the residences of many dignitaries and cultural celebrities, and there are many remains such as Qi Baishi's former residence and Wanrong's former residence. Nowadays, traditional houses and specialty shops are integrated in the alley, which not only retains the urban atmosphere of old Beijing, but also brings together cultural and creative vitality, making it a good place to experience Beijing-style hutong culture.
