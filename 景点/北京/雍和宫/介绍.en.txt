Yonghegong was originally the residence of Emperor Yongzheng before he came to the throne. During the Qianlong period, it was rebuilt into a royal temple of Tibetan Buddhism and became the center of the Qing Dynasty in charge of Tibetan Buddhist affairs across the country. The architecture integrates the styles of Han, Manchu, Mongolian and Tibetan ethnic groups, and the palace is majestic. It is especially famous for its white sandalwood Maitreya Buddha, which is more than 18 meters high. It is full of incense and is a special place for the capital to pray for blessings and worship Buddha.
