About 5300 to 4300 years ago, it was the center of power and belief of an early regional country in the late Neolithic Age. The ancient city is planned to have a triple centripetal structure of palace area, inner city, and outer city. Its peripheral water conservancy system is the earliest large-scale water conservancy project in China known so far. The "Jade Cong King" and the jade ritual system unearthed from Fanshan Cemetery in the city show the complex social hierarchy and unified beliefs. As a holy place that demonstrates the history of Chinese civilization for more than 5,000 years, it was included in the World Heritage List in 2019, filling the gap in the heritage of early urban civilizations in East Asia.
