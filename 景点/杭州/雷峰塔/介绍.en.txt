It was first built in the second year of Taiping and Xingguo in the Northern Song Dynasty (977). King Qian Chu of Wuyue built it to enshrine the Buddha's snail hair relics. The old tower collapsed in 1924, and Lu Xun wrote an article about it. The reconstruction was completed in 2002. The new tower is 71.7 meters high, and the ruins of the old tower are preserved for people to visit. The tower has five floors, with cornices and raised corners, and the interior is decorated with wood carvings of the story "The Legend of the White Snake". Climbing the tower overlooks the panoramic view of the West Lake, especially the "Leifeng Peak Sunset", one of the ten scenic spots of the West Lake. The shadow of the tower and the light of the lake complement each other in the twilight, which is full of poetry.
