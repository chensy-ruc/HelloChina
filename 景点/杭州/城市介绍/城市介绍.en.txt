Hangzhou is the capital of Zhejiang Province. It has been known as the "Paradise on Earth" since ancient times and has a history of more than 2,200 years. As a world cultural heritage, West Lake outlines the oriental aesthetic conception of "light makeup and heavy makeup are always suitable" with a lake of clear water and three clouds and mountains. Lingyin Temple, Liuhe Pagoda, Yuewang Temple and other cultural sites bear witness to the Wuyue culture and the legacy of the Southern Song Dynasty. Hangzhou is also the tea capital of China. Longjing tea is produced in the mountains of West Lake and its fragrance spreads far and wide. The Beijing-Hangzhou Grand Canal (Hangzhou Section) and the Qiantang River jointly carry the city’s water transportation memory. Modern Hangzhou is famous for its digital economy and is home to technology companies such as Alibaba, forming a city that emphasizes both historical heritage and innovative vitality. Traditional crafts such as silk, fans, and umbrella crafts continue to this day, showing the exquisite and elegant Jiangnan culture.
