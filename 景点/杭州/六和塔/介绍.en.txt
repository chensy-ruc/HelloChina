King Qian Hongchu of Wuyue built the garden to suppress the tide of the Qiantang River, and named it after the Buddhist "Six Harmonies". The existing tower is 59.89 meters tall, with an internal masonry structure of seven floors and an external wooden eaves with eight sides and thirteen floors. There are 104 iron bells hanging at the corners of the eaves. The sound of the bells can be heard for miles when the wind blows. The Xumi base in the pagoda is carved with exquisite patterns of flowers, birds, fairies and so on. Emperor Qianlong of the Qing Dynasty once inscribed plaques and characters on each floor. It was listed as one of the first batch of national key cultural relics protection units in 1961. You can overlook the Qiantang River Bridge and the vast river view from the tower. It is an outstanding representative of Hangzhou's Song Dynasty architecture.
