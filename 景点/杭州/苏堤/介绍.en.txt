In the fifth year of Yuanyou in the Northern Song Dynasty (1090), when Su Shi was appointed magistrate of Hangzhou, he dredged the West Lake and built a long embankment with lake mud and grass. It started from Nanping Mountain in the south and ended at Qixialing in the north, with a total length of nearly three kilometers. There are six bridges built on the embankment from south to north, including Yingbo, Suolan, Wangshan, Yadi, Dongpu and Kuahong bridges. They alternate with ups and downs and are called "Six Bridges of Yanliu". "Spring Dawn on Su Di" ranks first among the ten sceneries of West Lake. In early spring, willows line the bank, peach blossoms are scorching, and the morning light is shining, creating a picturesque artistic conception.
