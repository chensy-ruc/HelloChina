Founded in the first year of Xianhe in the Eastern Jin Dynasty (AD 326), it is the earliest famous temple in Hangzhou and one of the top ten ancient temples of Zen Buddhism in China. Backed by the North Peak and facing Feilai Peak, in front of the temple are more than 380 grotto statues from the Five Dynasties to the Yuan Dynasty, which are rare Buddhist art treasures in the south of the Yangtze River. The palace is laid out along the central axis. The statue of Sakyamuni in the Mahavira Hall is 19.6 meters high and is carved from 24 pieces of camphor wood. It has a wonderful and solemn appearance. The stone pagoda and sutra buildings of the Wuyue Kingdom in the temple are exquisitely carved and are listed as national key cultural relics protection units. It is a holy place for pilgrims and tourists at home and abroad.
